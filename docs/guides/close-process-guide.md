# Close Process Guide

## Purpose And Scope

This guide defines the controller close process for period close, reconciliation, consolidation, evidence retention, and audit packet assembly. It should be followed for monthly, quarterly, and fiscal year-end closes.

## Period Close

1. Confirm the active fiscal period and reporting basis.
2. Instantiate close task templates for the period.
3. Confirm task dependencies and due dates.
4. Assign preparers and reviewers.
5. Import or validate actuals.
6. Complete reconciliations and resolve exceptions.
7. Review intercompany matches and eliminations.
8. Run consolidation and review reports.
9. Assemble the audit packet.
10. Lock the fiscal period after signoff.

## Controls

- Period locks prevent uncontrolled updates after signoff.
- Reconciliations require preparer and reviewer workflow.
- Entity confirmations document campus-level ownership and responsibility.
- Elimination entries require review before inclusion in final reporting.
- Audit packets retain evidence links for later review.
- Reopening a period requires documented approval and audit logging.

## Reconciliation Workflow

Every reconciliation must have an account, period, preparer, reviewer, status, variance amount, aging status, and support when required. The preparer completes the reconciliation and attaches evidence. The reviewer checks reasonableness, completeness, and source support.

Exceptions should be categorized as timing, source system, mapping, missing evidence, or unresolved variance. Aging should be reviewed during every close meeting until cleared.

## Consolidation Workflow

Before consolidation:

1. Confirm entity hierarchy and ownership percentages.
2. Confirm currency and GAAP/book settings.
3. Confirm actuals and approved journals are posted.
4. Review intercompany matching.
5. Submit eliminations for review.

After consolidation, review consolidation audit reports, supplemental schedules, statutory packs, minority interest, CTA/currency translation, and before/after elimination effects.

## Audit Packet

Audit packets should include:

- Close checklist completion.
- Reconciliation status and evidence.
- Journal adjustments and approvals.
- Entity confirmations.
- Consolidation run summary.
- Elimination review history.
- Report snapshots and board package release references.
- Open exceptions and approved deferrals.

Do not release the packet until required evidence links resolve and the controller signs off.

## Close Calendar

Maintain a close calendar with due dates, dependency order, expected preparers, expected reviewers, and escalation contacts. When a task misses its due date, use workflow escalation rather than informal side-channel approval.

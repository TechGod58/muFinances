# Planner Guide

## Purpose And Scope

This guide is for department planners, budget office analysts, and finance staff who enter or review budgets, forecasts, comments, and supporting evidence. It assumes the user signs in through campus authentication and works inside a department-scoped workspace unless the budget office grants broader access.

## Planning Workflow

1. Sign in and confirm the active scenario, fiscal period, and department.
2. Open only the workspace sections needed for the task.
3. Review prior-year actuals, current budget, forecast, and scenario context.
4. Enter budget lines manually, paste rows into the editable grid, or import a prepared workbook.
5. Validate the grid and resolve inline errors.
6. Add comments and evidence for unusual changes, new requests, transfers, or one-time costs.
7. Submit the department budget for review.
8. Monitor notifications for requested changes or approvals.

## Data Entry

Budget lines require department, fund, account, period, amount, and notes. Use recurring expenses for repeated costs and one-time expenses for limited events or purchases. Use transfers only when the budget office expects funding to move between departments, funds, or accounts.

For bulk paste, keep headers consistent with the import template: `department_code`, `fund_code`, `account_code`, `period`, `amount`, and `notes`. Validate rows before submitting. If rows fail validation, correct the source values rather than overriding the check.

## Forecasts

Forecasts use typed drivers, historical actuals, seasonality, and scenario assumptions. After changing a driver:

1. Review driver lineage and dependency graph.
2. Run the forecast or predictive comparison.
3. Review confidence intervals and forecast-vs-actual variance.
4. Keep recommended changes in draft until a reviewer approves them.
5. Do not publish scenario changes until the budget office confirms the planning round is ready.

## Approvals And Evidence

Required explanations may be triggered by dollar or percentage thresholds. Provide plain-language explanations tied to the business reason, not just the account name. Attach source evidence when needed, such as quotes, grant notices, staffing approvals, or enrollment assumptions.

AI-drafted narratives remain drafts until a human approves them. Review source traces and confidence before using a draft in board or executive reporting.

## Review Screens

Use Missing Submissions to find departments that have not submitted. Use Department Comparison to identify outliers against the scenario average. Use notification center items to return to open tasks, comments, and approval requests.

## Planner Closeout

Before the planning deadline:

1. Confirm all department rows are submitted.
2. Resolve validation messages and missing comments.
3. Confirm attachments are readable.
4. Check that one-time items are not accidentally entered as recurring.
5. Save or export a copy of the submitted package if the department requires local records.
